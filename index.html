Polar Scientific Research Station: Why do we need a virtual socialized environment for next-generation embodied robot exploration under embodied Turing test? We develop a set of novel environment and present benchmark challenges specifically for robotic task, including:

  • rich interactive and realistic scenes,
  • multi perception simulation,
  • autonomous NPC,
  • spatiotemporal consistency and long horizon,
  • target-driven robotic task,
  • high scalability design,
  • a combination of scene understanding, learning, navigation, planning, and interaction with the environment.

We combine the variety and complexity of real-world with the flexibility of synthetic graphics and physics engines. We have constructed a social relationship context based on a semi-isolated micro-community for this environment, specifically the Polar Science Station . In this community, there are various common scenarios, from restaurants and medical rooms to work areas, where people perform their respective duties, carry out various activities to meet their needs, physiological or social, and achieve the goal of stable operation of the entire society. Compared to the design of physical environments, we emphasize that we start from people's basic needs to design so that their behavioral logic is reasonable and cognitive for robots. We have designed basic social behaviors to reflect social relationships. Meanwhile, people in the community often have their own preferences and proactive actions. This requires robots to understand social concepts.





Introduction

There are three floors and 12 NPC shaped buildings here. Various forms of robots possess incredible perceptual abilities, allowing them to control themselves to generate various behaviors. Our Purpose

Recently, a new form of AI has emerged in the AI community, embodied intelligence, which discusses perception, motion, reasoning, and planning as a whole. Compared to traditional paradigms in the past, embodied settings provide more proactive solutions, such as embodied navigation, embodied Q&A, and language behavior tasks that integrate language, vision, and action. It has been proven that embodied settings are not simply the sum of settings. When viewed as a whole, these abilities can interact to achieve goals that were previously difficult to achieve in a single setting. This opens up new paradigms for the AI community and provides new possible business scenarios and technological routes for the industry. However, even though embodied intelligence has achieved certain accomplishments, this field still faces paradigm problems. For example, although we now enable robots to take active action, it is still difficult to respond to various events as flexibly as animals, and adapt to and understand the surrounding environment, which is constrained by existing learning paradigms and computational efficiency. Alternatively, although we can parse language instructions through large models and perform well in some well-designed real-world scenarios, the performance of these features in diverse and unpredictable specific family scenarios with personal characteristics is difficult to promise. Existing methods cannot quickly adjust their behavior based on the facts of the scene, and it is also difficult to learn on-site. Therefore, although embodied intelligence provides us with a good start, the next step is an open question of how to move forward.
